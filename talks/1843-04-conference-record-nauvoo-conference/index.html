Minutes and Discourses of the April 1843 Nauvoo General Conference

Conference record

1843 April General Conference·Whole Conference·1843-04-06

Source Text

Source note: Joseph Smith, History, 1838-1856, volume D-1 [1 August 1842-1 July 1843], pages 1512-1523. JSP describes the volume as created after Joseph Smith's death, with page 1512 in the handwriting of Thomas Bullock. The April 1843 conference record begins in the 6 April entry on page 1512 and continues through the 9 April entry on page 1523. ¶

This file is an umbrella conference record. JSP also preserves separate discourse witnesses from the same conference, including Joseph Smith discourses on 6 April and 8 April 1843; those should be represented as separate talk files only when ingested from their discrete JSP discourse pages. This record keeps the conference minutes together and does not split summarized remarks by Orson Pratt, Brigham Young, Hyrum Smith, Joseph Smith, Joshua Grant, or others into artificial standalone talks. ¶

Excerpted Conference Record

6 April 1843. "The first day of the 14th year of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints." The weather was "clear, warm and pleasant"; the Mississippi ice was still thick enough that many crossed from Iowa "to the conference, on the ice"; and the walls of the temple were "from 4 to 12 feet above the floor." ¶

"At annual Conference of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints," the meeting "was convened on the floor of the Temple." The record lists Hyrum Smith, Brigham Young, Heber C. Kimball, Orson Pratt, Wilford Woodruff, John Taylor, George A. Smith, Willard Richards, Amasa Lyman, "and a very large assembly of the elders and saints." ¶

Brigham Young announced that Joseph Smith was detained on business. After hymns and prayer, Orson Pratt read 2 Peter 3 and spoke on the resurrection. Joseph Smith arrived with Sidney Rigdon and Orson Hyde shortly before noon. ¶

Joseph Smith opened by expressing gratitude for the Saints' privilege of meeting together, then stated the objects of the conference: first, "to ascertain the standing of the first presidency"; second, "to take into consideration the expediency of sending out the Twelve, or some of them, amongst the branches of the church, to obtain stock to build the Nauvoo House"; and third, to hear appeals from other conferences if any existed. ¶

Joseph Smith asked whether the conference sustained him as president of the church. The motion was carried unanimously. Sidney Rigdon was then presented as counselor and sustained almost unanimously; William Law was sustained as second counselor; and Hyrum Smith was sustained as Patriarch. ¶

Much of the first day's business concerned how funds for the Temple and Nauvoo House should be gathered and accounted for. Joseph Smith argued that the Twelve were the proper agents to collect means, that funds should be reported to the Trustee in Trust, and that traveling expenses should not be taken from funds collected for the houses. The conference voted that the Twelve collect funds for the Nauvoo House and receive moneys for the Temple, with bond and reporting requirements. ¶

In the afternoon, Hyrum Smith warned the conference against stealing and secret combinations. Joseph Smith continued the subject, saying that stealing had never been tolerated by the First Presidency and that elders should make that clear abroad. ¶

On 7 April, the record says conference business closed and the remainder of the time was devoted to instruction. Orson Pratt delivered a discourse "from the Prophecy of Daniel on the Amount of Days"; Joseph Smith remarked that "this day is a Millenium, it is a Millenium within these walls, for there is nothing but peace." ¶

On 8 April, Joseph Smith addressed the Saints. The record says the synopsis was reported by Willard Richards and William Clayton. He asked the congregation to pray that the wind would calm, that his lungs would be strengthened, and that the Holy Ghost would rest upon him. He then spoke on the beasts in Revelation, partly to reduce contention and speculation among the elders. The discourse is a discrete JSP discourse witness and should be ingested separately if represented as a Joseph Smith talk. ¶

On 9 April, the meeting opened with "The spirit of God like a fire is burning." Because Joseph Smith was afflicted in his lungs and breast, he called on Joshua Grant to speak. Grant reported on a three-year mission during which he and his brother Jedediah had raised up a congregation of about 200 members. Amasa Lyman then delivered remarks on the Book of Mormon, resurrection of the dead, and eternal judgment. ¶
