Spiritual Communication

Parley P. Pratt · 1853 April General Conference · 1853-04-06

Fidelity

◑Contemporaneous report

Delivered April 6, 1853, at the Northeast cornerstone of the Salt Lake Temple. This is the major NE cornerstone oration; Hyde offered the NE prayer (filed separately). Pratt's discourse addresses spiritual communication through authorized priesthood channels versus the spiritualism and mesmerism popular in American culture at the time, arguing that the Temple is the proper conduit for communication between heaven and earth. Published in Journal of Discourses, Volume 2, pp. 43-47 (confirmed via the weebly bibliography and cross-checked against jod.mrm.org); this file's text is complete and self-contained, ending in its own closing 'Amen.' A second, separately titled 'Spiritual Communication' discourse by Pratt was delivered the next day (April 7, JD 1:6-15) as part of the regular Tabernacle sessions — a distinct discourse on the same theme, not a continuation of this text, and not yet filed in this vault.
