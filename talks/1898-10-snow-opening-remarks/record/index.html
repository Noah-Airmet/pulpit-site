Opening Remarks

Lorenzo Snow · 1898 October General Conference · 1898-10-06

Fidelity

◕Authoritative print

Transcribed from the OCR of the official printed Conference Report of the Sixty-ninth Semi-Annual Conference (October 1898), scanned and hosted at the Internet Archive (conferencereport1898sa). OCR was cleaned and corrected: running heads, page numbers, and line-break hyphenation removed; obvious OCR errors fixed. Not yet proofread page-by-page against the scan images, so minor residual OCR errors may remain (status: draft, pending review).

Heavyweight pass: source URL verified (archive.org conferencereport1898sa, IA djvu OCR). Page-by-page collation found this talk was truncated mid-sentence ('Now, in some respects we differ from') at the previous ingestion's page break; the OCR's page-break running head at that point was misread/mis-set as 'PRESIDENT GEORGE Q. CANNON,' which caused the sentence's completion and closing two paragraphs ('any other class of people...Amen') to be wrongly filed under a separate Cannon talk. Restored those three paragraphs here as the talk's real ending (confirmed by content: Snow, the presiding officer, closes by praying for the conference and turning the meeting over to 'the brethren'). Also removed two page-header OCR artifacts ('2 GENERAL C' / 'OhFERENCE', a garbled 'GENERAL CONFERENCE' running head split across a page break) that were interrupting sentences mid-word, and fixed OCR typos (alwTays->always, antideluvians->antediluvians, dene->done, Latter-dav->Latter-day, and similar). See companion note on 1898-10-cannon-missionary-labor-and-self-reliance.md, the newly split-out file for Cannon's actual, distinct discourse that had been wrongly merged into this one.
