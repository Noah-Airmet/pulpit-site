And Now My Brethren And Sisters...

David O. McKay · 1968 October General Conference · 1968-10-06

Fidelity

◕Authoritative print

Transcribed from the official printed Conference Report of the One Hundred Thirty-eighth Semiannual (October 1968) General Conference, digitized by the Church History Library and hosted at the Internet Archive (item conferencereport1968sa), pp. 144-148. Reading order rebuilt from the item's DjVu OCR word coordinates (scripts/ingest/cr-reading-order.py). Never previously ingested into the vault; flagged missing by the 2026 heavyweight review of this conference and ingested in this pass. President McKay, then 95 and under doctor's orders to remain at his apartment, did not deliver this address in person; per President Alvin R. Dyer's introduction, "President David O. McKay has asked his son, Elder Robert R. McKay, to read you his closing message for this conference," matching the printed calling line "(Read by his son Robert R. McKay)." This is the closing address of the conference, immediately following Elder Howard W. Hunter's testimony address (already in the vault) and ending with the conference's benediction announcements (administrative, omitted from this transcript). Running heads, page numbers, and leaf markers removed; de-hyphenated line breaks and reflowed paragraphs; printed editorial side-heads preserved as Markdown headings without altering wording.
