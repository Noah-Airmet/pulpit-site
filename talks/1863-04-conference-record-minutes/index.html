Minutes of the April 1863 General Conference

Conference record

1863 April General Conference·Whole Conference·1863-04-06

The Thirty-Third Annual Conference of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints, convened in the Bowery, Great Salt Lake City, April 6-7, 1863, President Brigham Young presiding. ¶

The morning session of April 6 opened with President Daniel H. Wells' "Building the Temple and a New Tabernacle—Labor Tithing—Call for Faithful Laborers" discourse (filed separately), followed by President Heber C. Kimball, who recounted his early missionary travels and the building of the Kirtland Temple, and gave extensive counsel on temple endowments, missionary calls, and the support of missionaries' families ("Building the Temple—Endowments—Counsel to Missionaries, Etc.," JD 10:163-169; filed separately). Elder George A. Smith followed with "Missionary Fund—Support of the Families of Elders Who Are on Missions" (JD 10:143-145; filed separately), describing the changed circumstances of missionary travel since the early English mission. ¶

The afternoon session heard from Elder John Taylor on home manufactures and the gathering of the poor, then his fuller discourse "The Blessings the Saints Will Enjoy—How the Kingdom of God is to Be Established—Building Temples, Tabernacles, and Houses—Gathering the Poor" (JD 10:145-151; filed separately). ¶

Additional discourses from this conference, all filed separately as full talks, include Ezra T. Benson's "Practical Duties of the Saints—Blessings Resulting From Their Performance" (April 6, JD 10:151-154), Charles C. Rich's "Building the Temple—General Duties of the Saints" (April 7, JD 10:160-163), Orson Hyde's "Destruction of Babylon—How the Saints Are to Become Saviors of the World" (April 7, JD 10:155-159), and Amasa M. Lyman's "Advice to Missionaries—Preaching the Gospel—Gathering the Poor, Etc." (April 7, JD 10:178-187). ¶
