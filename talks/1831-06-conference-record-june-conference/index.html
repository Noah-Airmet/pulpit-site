Minutes of the June 1831 Conference

Conference record

1831 June General Conference·Whole Conference·1831-06-03

Source note

Preferred witness: Minute Book 2, pages 3–4, accessed 21 May 2026. The minutes preserve proceedings for 3 and 6 June, including exhortation references, ordinations, and brief reported remarks. ¶

Additional witness: History, 1838–1856, volume A-1, page 118, accessed 19 May 2026. The later history summarizes the conference's harmony, manifestations, and ordinations. ¶

The transcripts below follow the Joseph Smith Papers expanded-transcription presentation: original spelling and punctuation are retained; editorial additions appear in brackets; insertions appear in angle brackets; cancellations appear with strikethrough; and manuscript page endings are linked to the corresponding JSP image and transcript page. ¶

Historical introduction

The June 1831 conference began in Kirtland on 3 June and reconvened on 6 June. The record is especially significant for the first distinctive ordinations to the high priesthood, but its remarks and exhortations survive only in minute form. ¶

This material remains one conference record because the surviving sources preserve proceedings, retrospective summaries, or reported remarks rather than a set of discrete, complete sermon transcripts. ¶

History, 1838–1856, volume A-1

On the 6th of June, the elders from the various parts of the country where they were laboring, came in and the conference before appointed, convened in Kirtland, and the Lord displayed his power <​to the most perfect satisfaction, of the saints​> in a manner that could not be mistaken. The man of sin was revealed, and the au[HC 1:175]thority of the melechisedec <​priesthood​> was manifested and <​I​> conferred, <​the high priesthood​> for the first time, upon several of the elders. It was clearly evidint that the Lord gave us power in proportion to the work to be done and strength according to the race set before us; and grace and help as our needs required. Great harmony prevailed. Several were ordained; Faith was strengthened; [HC 1:176] and humility, so necessary for the blessing of God to follow prayer, characterized the saints. ¶

Notes from Tyler’s compilation

D&C 52 was received June 6, 1831, after the conference had been adjourned earlier that day. From the introduction to that section: ¶

“At this conference the first distinctive ordinations to the office of high priest were made, and certain manifestations of false and deceiving spirits were discerned and rebuked.” ¶

(D&C 52, Introduction) ¶
