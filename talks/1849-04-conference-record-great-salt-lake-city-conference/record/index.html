The First General Epistle, Reporting the April 1849 Great Salt Lake City General Conference

Conference record · 1849 April General Conference · 1849-04-06

Fidelity

○Fragmentary

No minutes of this conference have been located. The only substantive witness found is the First Presidency's 'First General Epistle,' composed shortly after the April 6-7, 1849 conference and reporting it in a single short paragraph embedded in a much longer administrative and settlement report; the epistle itself states the conference did no independent business beyond sustaining 'the several officers mentioned in this epistle' -- i.e., the appointments and organizations described earlier in the same document. Transcribed from the clean reprint in Millennial Star, Vol. XI, No. 14 (July 15, 1849), pp. 227-232, itself reprinted from the New York Herald (June 22, 1849). The conference date (April 6, 1849) matches indexes/conference-calendar.md; the epistle's own composition date is not given more precisely than 'the annual conference... closed on Sabbath' (April 8, 1849) plus a few days to prepare for the mail. Graded fragmentary: this is not a record of conference proceedings so much as a document that reports a conference happened and briefly summarizes its one piece of business.

Witnesses

Frontier Guardian, April 18, 1849, Vol. 1, No. 6, p. 2archived

A short, independent eyewitness item ('CONFERENCE.') describing the weather and crowding at the conference, printed 12 days after it closed -- not another copy of the First General Epistle. Open to research. Not made the preferred witness because it is shorter than the Epistle and does not describe the conference's business (sustaining votes, mission assignments); it supplements rather than replaces the Epistle as this file's main source.

Millennial Star, Vol. XI, No. 14 (July 15, 1849), pp. 227-232 (reprinted from the New York Herald, June 22, 1849)transcription sourcearchived

OCR'd from MStar_Vol_11_djvu.txt (lines ~19506-19945). This OCR is unusually clean for this vault's early-restoration Millennial Star sources; only minor mechanical fixes were needed (e.g. 'Uanvin' left as printed elsewhere in the issue is unrelated text, not part of this epistle). Full text of the First General Epistle is included below, not only the conference-specific paragraph, because the epistle itself frames its entire officer/organization report as what the conference voted to sustain.

Frontier Guardian, May 30, 1849, Vol. 1, No. 9archived

Same document (the First General Epistle), printed six weeks earlier than the Millennial Star's reprint, but this issue's OCR on archive.org is far more degraded (broken column interleaving, many garbled words) and was not used as the transcription base. Confirms the Millennial Star text is a faithful reprint (matching passages checked include the officer lists and the conference paragraph) and could be collated against a page image in a future pass.
