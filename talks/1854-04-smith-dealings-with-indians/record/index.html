Dealings with Indians—Defence of Territorial Measures

George A. Smith · 1854 April General Conference · 1854-04-06

Fidelity

◑Contemporaneous report

Published in Deseret News, May 11, 1854, p. 49. Smith's discourse addressed the Walker War (1853-1854 Indian conflict) and defended the defensive measures taken by Utah settlers. He argued that the measures adopted were not oppressive, comparing them to conflicts in other frontier regions, and used examples from Eastern frontier Indian conflicts to provide context. Unlike the three other April 1854 conference discourses (Young, Kimball, Hyde), this one was published in the Deseret News rather than the Journal of Discourses. Full text not yet reproduced here — check Deseret News, May 11, 1854, p. 49 for the complete text.
