CANNON.' with the subhead 'Condition of missionary fields—Preparation for the ministry—Relating to immigration—Providing employment—efficacy of faith'). This is a newly split-out file: the prior ingestion had merged the tail end of Lorenzo Snow's opening remarks (the paragraphs beginning 'any other class of people...') together with this, Cannon's own distinct discourse, under the old id 1898-10-cannon-ancient-and-modern-revelation with a title ('The Scriptures and Living Revelation') that matched neither half. Confirmed via the OCR that Snow's opening remarks are truncated mid-sentence at a page break, and the page-break running head was misread as Cannon's name, causing the ingestion script to split there instead of at the real discourse boundary (which is marked by 'Amen' followed immediately by a repeated speaker header plus topic subhead -- the pattern for a genuine new discourse in this volume, as opposed to a bare running-head repeat mid-sentence). Moved Snow's true ending back to 1898-10-snow-opening-remarks.md; this file now contains only Cannon's actual discourse (the 'Condition of missionary fields' address), retitled and re-ID'd accordingly. Collated the body against the OCR paragraph by paragraph: fixed OCR errors (timeprobably->time, probably; called On a mission->called on a mission; youngmen->young men; net uncommon->not uncommon; tnd->and; att^rded->attended; thi trials->the trials; ar^|->and; Trusteein-Trust->Trustee-in-Trust; stray capitalization/quote-mark artifacts). No missing text; boundaries confirmed against Cowley's discourse (which follows immediately, First Day Morning talk_order 3).
