Two short phrases remain genuinely undecipherable in the source OCR itself even after inference: 'repre- [[gap]] our faith' (leaf 126) is rendered here as 'represent our faith', which is the only sensible completion of the visible letters and matches the sentence's grammar, though a stray printer's mark obscures the exact original spacing/hyphenation; and 'who will listen, frequently, [[gap]] the testimonies that are borne' has an untranscribable OCR dropout of one or two words between 'frequently' and 'the testimonies' -- left as an ellipsis-free best reading of 'to the testimonies' since the surrounding grammar requires a preposition there and no plausible alternative reading changes the sentence's meaning; both are flagged here rather than silently invented.
