Minutes of the April 1877 General Conference (St. George)

Conference record

1877 April General Conference·Whole Conference·1877-04-06

Minutes of the Forty-Seventh Annual Conference of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, convened at the St. George Temple, St. George, Utah, April 6-8, 1877. President Brigham Young presiding. This was a historically significant conference: the St. George Temple — the first Latter-day Saint temple dedicated in the mountains — provided the venue for all sessions. ¶

First Day Morning Session: President Daniel H. Wells delivered [[1877-04-wells-dedicatory-prayer]], the temple's dedicatory prayer, consecrating all building components and requesting divine protection and blessing for those who would worship there. Elder George Q. Cannon spoke on union in temporal affairs as essential to salvation (summary only). President Brigham Young then delivered [[1877-04-young-the-united-orderthe-duty-of-the]] (both filed separately), arguing that proper implementation of the United Order required teaching people financial stewardship rather than simply dividing property equally. ¶

First Day Afternoon Session: Elder Joseph F. Smith spoke on temple worship preparation and the principle that opposition is proportional to faithfulness. President John W. Young and President Brigham Young also spoke (all summary only). ¶

Second Day Morning Session: Elder Charles C. Rich spoke on covenant responsibility, and Elder Wilford Woodruff spoke on Joseph Smith's priesthood keys and Brigham Young's leadership role. Missionary calls were read, and Elder George Q. Cannon and President Brigham Young also spoke (all summary only). ¶

Second Day Afternoon Session: Missionary calls were read, the General Authorities of the Church were sustained by vote of the Conference, and Elder Franklin D. Richards spoke on the Nauvoo Temple. Elder Erastus Snow spoke on the United Order, followed by further missionary calls (all summary only). ¶

Third Day (April 8): Elder Brigham Young, Jr. spoke, closing the conference (summary only; never printed in full in JD). ¶

Themes: United Order implementation through priesthood direction, self-sufficiency, the St. George Temple's sacred dedicatory events, settlement of additional valleys, defense of schooling practices. Over 100 missionaries called to Europe, Arizona, and the United States; fourteen elders called to study Spanish. Joseph F. Smith appointed to lead the European Mission from Liverpool. Relief Societies sustained to promote silk production. General Authorities unanimously sustained. ¶
