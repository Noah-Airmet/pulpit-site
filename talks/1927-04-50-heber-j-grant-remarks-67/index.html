Session Business Closing the Fourth Day Morning Meeting and Opening the Concluding Meeting

Conference record

1927 April General Conference·Fourth Day Morning Meeting / Fourth Day Afternoon Meeting (Concluding Meeting)·1927-04-06

President Charles H. Hart's remarks about the Catholic Encyclopedia have brought the following memorandum from Brother Orson F. Whitney: ¶

"President Grant: I wrote the article on 'Mormons' for the new Catholic Encyclopedia, by request of President Joseph F. Smith, and at the solicitation of Dean Harris. He signed it, and sent it to New York, where it was changed, things being written into it that the article, as it left my hands, did not contain. The assertion that the Three Witnesses denied their Testimony was one of these interpolations. I protested to the Dean, and he invited me to correct the misstatement, which I did. Whether they published my second article in the Encyclopedia (as promised) I know not. (Signed) Orson F. Whitney." ¶

Apparently they did not. ¶

The congregation sang, "Do what is right." ¶

The closing prayer was offered by Elder Joseph E. Cordon. ¶

Conference adjourned until 2 p. m. ¶

CONCLUDING MEETING ¶

The closing meeting of the 97th Annual Conference session was held on Wednesday, April 6, 1927, at 2 o'clock, in the tabernacle, a large congregation being in attendance. ¶

President Heber J. Grant presided, and announced that the congregation would sing the hymn, "Now let us rejoice in the day of salvation." ¶

After singing, prayer was offered by Elder George H. Brimhall. ¶

The congregation sang, "Zion stands with hills surrounded." ¶
