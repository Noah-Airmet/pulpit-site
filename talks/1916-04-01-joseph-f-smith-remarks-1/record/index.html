Fixed a genuine column-interleaving OCR bug on leaf 7 (source page 7): the financial-report paragraph beginning 'every action in life...' through '...December 31, 1915:' had been transcribed by reading the two printed columns line-by-line left-then-right ('every action in life, will be a ser- brethren, I do not wish to claim any'), producing two spliced non-sequiturs instead of one paragraph; de-interleaved by reading the left column top-to-bottom then the right column top-to-bottom, which restored a single coherent paragraph ('...a sermon to the unwary...' / '...or more. The report which follows...'). Reflowed the itemized financial report into readable sentences (source is a simple one-column tally list, not a bug, just OCR line-breaks), fixed OCR letter errors ('T do not wish'->'I do not wish', '39<$'->'39%', 'Gol bless'->'God bless', '1,555.000.00'->'1,555,000.00', '600.000.00'->'600,000.00' — comma/period confusion consistent with the surrounding thousands-separated figures), and dropped stray running-artifact fragments ('. . .- f. „', a lone apostrophe) that are OCR misreads of table dot-leaders, not text. No wording invented; every dollar figure and statistic is the OCR's own digits, just rejoined and re-ordered. Confirmed this talk is genuinely complete, opening as the conference's Opening Address and closing 'Amen' before the following soprano solo (correctly a separate item, not part of this file).
