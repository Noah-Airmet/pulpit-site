Minutes of the April 1873 General Conference

Conference record · 1873 April General Conference · 1873-04-06

Fidelity

○Fragmentary

Deseret News minutes of the 43rd Annual Conference, with sessions April 6-8, 1873, and additional sessions May 3-4, 1873. Approximately 8,000-10,000 in attendance. First Presidency: Brigham Young, Daniel H. Wells (George A. Smith on Palestine pilgrimage). All 5 discourses with surviving JD full text from the April 6-7 sessions are filed separately as talks: Brigham Young's 'Friends and Enemies...' (Sunday April 6 PM); Orson Hyde's 'Testimony—Sickness in Sanpete County...' and Brigham Young's 'The Order of Enoch...' (Monday April 7 AM, with Orson Pratt's 'Establishment of the Kingdom of God...' also that morning); and Brigham Young's 'Assistance of the Ladies...' and Wilford Woodruff's 'Home Manufacture...' (Monday April 7 PM) — confirmed against the Weebly page's own bibliography, which lists no other JD citations for this conference. The many other named speakers (Taylor, Cannon's several appearances, Smoot, and the May 3-4 continuation speakers: Brigham Young Jr., Joseph F. Smith, McKenzie, Rich, Joseph Young) are recorded only as third-person Deseret News summaries, with no full JD text located — consistent with the bibliography, not a vault gap.
