Minutes of the October 1857 General Conference

Conference record · 1857 October General Conference · 1857-10-06

Fidelity

○Fragmentary

Deseret News minutes of the October 6-7, 1857 General Conference. Clerk: Leo Hawkins. Reporter: J. V. Long. All six discourses from this conference that survive in the Journal of Discourses are filed separately as full talks: Brigham Young's 'Advantages of Trials and Experience' and 'Testimony of the Spirit,' Lorenzo Snow's 'Wisdom Gained By Experience,' Charles C. Rich's 'Sufficiency of the Gospel,' Amasa M. Lyman's 'Peace, Confidence, and Ultimate Victory of the Saints,' and Erastus Snow's 'Testimonies of Returned Missionaries.' Corrected during heavyweight review; a prior pass of this note did not cross-reference Lyman, Erastus Snow, or Young's second discourse even though they already existed as separate files. The many shorter testimonies from returned missionaries remain summarized here rather than filed separately. This conference took place while Johnston's Army was marching toward Utah Territory.
