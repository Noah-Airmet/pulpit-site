Transcription status

This file preserves a complete transcription of Thomas Bullock's 22-leaf "General Conference minutes, Iowa" (CR 100 318), Historian's Office general Church minutes, covering the April 6-9, 1848 General Conference at the Log Tabernacle, Kanesville (Council Bluffs), Iowa. Leaves are numbered 1-22 in catalog-viewer order (image files CR 100 318_2_5_3.jpg through _2_5_24.jpg); Bullock's own page numbers, where present, are noted at the head of each leaf. Leaf 11 is a half-sheet team-subscription list; leaves 12 and 22 are docket wrappers. ¶

Collation notes

  • "The Log Tabernacle" is the Kanesville/Council Bluffs meetinghouse; the settlement was formally named Kanesville by O. Hyde's motion recorded on leaf 18 (April 8).
  • This conference ratified the same First Presidency reorganization first decided by the Twelve on December 5, 1847 and ratified at the December 27, 1847 Council Bluffs conference (see 1847-12-conference-record-council-bluffs-sustaining-conference.md) and again at the October 8, 1848 Great Salt Lake Valley conference (see 1848-10-conference-record-great-salt-lake-valley-conference.md).
  • The First General Epistle's one-paragraph recollection of this conference (secondary witness above) is consistent with the manuscript's fuller record of the sustaining.
  • The speaker of the leaf 2-4 discourse is unlabeled in the manuscript; internal evidence (third-person references to "Bro Hyde," Carthage, "Uncle Sid," "Bogus Brigham") points to Brigham Young, but the transcription leaves the attribution in a note rather than the text.
