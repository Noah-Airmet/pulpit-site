Historical introduction

The conference met in Kirtland, Ohio, on 3 May 1834. Its proceedings included the formal adoption of the name “The Church of the Latter Day Saints,” instructions concerning church governance, and reports from branches represented at the meeting. ¶

This material remains one conference record because the surviving source intermingles proceedings, reported remarks, and institutional business rather than preserving a set of discrete, complete sermon transcripts. ¶

Transcript

Kirtland, Ohio, May 3, 1834. ¶

MINUTES of a Conference of the Elders of the church of Christ, which church was organized in the township of Fayette, Seneca county, New-York, on the 6th of April, A. D. 1830. ¶

The Conference came to order, and Joseph Smith Jr. was chosen Moderator, and Frederick G. Williams and Oliver Cowdery, were appointed clerks. ¶

After prayer the Conference proceeded to discuss the subject of names and appellations, when a motion was made by Sidney Rigdon,1 and seconded by Newel K. Whitney, that this church be known hereafter by the name of THE CHURCH OF THE LATTER DAY SAINTS. Appropriate remarks were delivered by some of the members, after which the motion was put by the Moderator, and passed by unanimous voice. ¶

Resolved that this Conference recommend to the Conferences and Churches abroad,2 that in making out and transmitting Minutes of their proceedings, such minutes and proceedings be made out under the above title. ¶

Resolved that these Minutes be signed by the Moderator and Clerks, and published in The Evening and The Morning Star. ¶

JOSEPH SMITH JR. Moderator.
Frederick G. Williams, Clerks.
Oliver Cowdery.

Textual and contextual notes

Notes

  1. David Whitmer, who was living in Missouri in 1834, later said that changing the church’s name came solely at Sidney Rigdon’s instigation. Because the minutes capture none of the discussion surrounding the change, the extent of Rigdon’s and others’ advocacy for the change is unknown. (Whitmer, Address to All Believers in Christ, 73.) Whitmer, David. An Address to All Believers in Christ. Richmond, MO: By the author, 1887. ↩

  2. That is, those branches outside of the Kirtland, Ohio, and Missouri areas. ↩
