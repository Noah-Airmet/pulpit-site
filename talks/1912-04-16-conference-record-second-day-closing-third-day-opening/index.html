Session notes: anthem introduction, Second Day closing, and Third Day (Easter) opening

Conference record

1912 April General Conference·Second Day Afternoon Session·1912-04-06

Elder B. H. Roberts introduced the next musical number: the anthem "The Lord Hath Brought Again Zion," with words from Doctrine and Covenants 84:99-102, composed by Brother Arthur Shepherd, who set the passage to music after his attention was called to its poetic character. The anthem is in two parts, a recitative solo followed by a chorus. The choir then rendered the anthem, with Prof. Charles R. Johnson singing the tenor solo parts. ¶

President Joseph F. Smith thanked the Brigham Young University Choir, under the direction of Brother Anthon C. Lund, for the music furnished during the day's two sessions. ¶

Benediction was pronounced by Elder Joseph R. Shepherd. ¶

Conference adjourned until Sunday, April 7, 10 a.m. ¶

In the Tabernacle, Sunday, April 7, 10 a.m. The stand was decorated in white bunting interspersed with green leaves, with pots of Easter lilies added to the decoration. ¶

Conference was called to order by President Joseph F. Smith, who announced that an overflow meeting would convene that morning in the Assembly Hall for those who could not be accommodated in the overcrowded Tabernacle. ¶

The Tabernacle choir sang the anthem "Unfold Ye Portals, Everlasting." ¶

Prayer was offered by Elder Wm. H. Smart. ¶

An Easter anthem, "Christ Is Risen," words and music by Prof. Evan Stephens, was rendered by the choir; Sister Rose Smithen and Brother Carl Samuelson sang the solos and duets. ¶

President Joseph F. Smith remarked that "Christ Is Risen" was the latest production of Evan Stephens, and expressed the hope that it would by no means be his last. ¶
