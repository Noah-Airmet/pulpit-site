The Political Rule of the Church (Political Manifesto)

Heber J. Grant, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles

1896 April General Conference·Monday Afternoon Session·1896-04-06

To the Officers and Members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, in General Conference assembled: ¶

Dear Brethren and Sisters: ¶

Every Latter-day Saint will recognize the value of union, not only in action but in matters of faith and discipline. As to the rights and authority of the Priesthood of the Son of God, it is of the highest importance that there should be no difference of opinion among the officers and members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. We have felt led to address you upon this subject in consequence of recent discussions which have arisen among the people. ¶

It has always been the desire of the Church to maintain the separation of Church and State, and to leave citizens entirely free to exercise their political rights according to their own conscience and judgment. At the same time, when officers of the Church are chosen to occupy leading positions that require their full time and attention in the ministry, it is our rule and practice that before accepting nominations or candidacy for political office or other distracting secular vocations, they should consult with their presiding officers and obtain their counsel and consent. This rule is necessary in order that the work of the Lord may not be neglected, and that harmony and union may be maintained among the leaders of the people. ¶

We therefore submit this rule and principle to the congregation of the Saints for your sustaining vote and endorsement. ¶

WILFORD WOODRUFF, GEORGE Q. CANNON, JOSEPH F. SMITH, First Presidency. ¶
