Heavyweight pass: verified against archive.org/details/conferencereport1912a (internal scan conferencereport821chur), leaves 66-71 (page/n66, running head 'ELDER JOSEPH F. SMITH, JR.'). Confirmed boundaries: this is the Third Day (Easter Sunday, April 7, 1912) Morning Session's first address, begins immediately after Elder Anthony W. Ivins's address and a baritone solo ('Hosannah,' Elder Horace S. Ensign), opening with a quotation from D&C 18:10-15 on the worth of souls; discusses the resurrection witnesses and the Genealogical Society of Utah (organized 1894 under President Wilford Woodruff); ends '...give them courage to resist and overcome the world, and do all things in righteousness required of them, is my prayer, in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen.' A stray 'ELDER CHARLES A. CALLIS.' running head bleeds onto the final leaf while Smith's own text is still concluding underneath it -- a page-proof artifact, excluded, confirmed by reading past it to Callis's true byline, calling note, and opening remarks. De-hyphenated, reflowed, corrected scattered OCR errors, and removed the printed synopsis block per docs/review.md. Speaker identified as Joseph Fielding Smith (later Church President), styled 'Joseph F. Smith, Jr.' in this conference report to distinguish him from his father, President Joseph F. Smith.
