Minutes of the April 1850 Great Salt Lake City General Conference

Conference record · 1850 April General Conference · 1850-04-06

Fidelity

○Fragmentary

Transcribed from the Millennial Star, Vol. XII, No. 17 (Sept. 1, 1850), pp. 257-262 and No. 18 (Sept. 15, 1850), pp. 273-277, which together reprinted the minutes of the April 6-8, 1850 Great Salt Lake City General Conference. Follow-up (2026-07-16, first pass): confirmed via Utah Digital Newspapers that the Deseret News's first issue is dated June 15, 1850 -- more than two months after this conference -- so no contemporary Deseret News printing of these minutes can exist; the previously listed 'Deseret News, April 1850' witness was based on a mistaken assumption and has been removed. Follow-up (2026-07-16, second pass): located a second contemporary witness, the Frontier Guardian (Kanesville, Iowa), whose full-text OCR on Internet Archive contains the same conference minutes -- likely faster to reach print than the England-based Millennial Star's September reprint. Its scan is column-scrambled (OCR interleaves adjacent newspaper columns line by line), but legible fragments across the scrambled lines resolve the previously illegible Twelve Apostles sustaining-vote gap: the fragment sequence (Patriarch vote, then 'Quo[rum]...ried; also [P.P. Pratt]...Woodruff...Amasa Lym[a]n...[C.C.] Rich, Loren[zo Snow]...Franklin D. [Richards]...same; carr[ied]') matches, name for name and in the same order, the verbatim-confirmed September 1850 sustaining vote for the same Quorum of the Twelve already transcribed in the companion 1850-09 conference_record ('Orson Hyde was sustained as President, and P. P. Pratt, O. Pratt, W. Woodruff, J. Taylor, G. A. Smith, A. Lyman, E. T. Benson, C. C. Rich, L. Snow, E. Snow, and F. D. Richards, as members of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles'). The body below now gives that reconstructed vote using the September vote's confirmed wording, clearly flagged as a reconstruction rather than a clean read of either witness -- not every name is independently legible in the April scan's fragments, but none contradicts the September pattern and several (Woodruff, Amasa Lyman, Rich, Lorenzo Snow, Franklin D. Richards) are directly confirmed in the fragments. The Frontier Guardian's report does not appear to include the Sunday morning discourse at the length the Millennial Star gives it, so the second flagged gap (the unattributed George A. Smith-to-Brigham-Young speaker shift) remains unresolved -- searched for the transition phrase and did not find it in this witness. Graded fragmentary per schema.md's rule that conference_record entries are never verbatim.

Two passages are OCR-damaged beyond a confident mechanical fix and are marked in place: (1) the sustaining vote for the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, which collapses into unreadable fragments ('Mov / carried / A / Si') between the Patriarch's sustaining vote and Willard Richards's -- see the Frontier Guardian witness below, which resolves this; (2) a stretch of the Sunday, April 7 morning session where the first-person voice shifts from what reads as George A. Smith's opening remarks to language ('your humble servant at their head' of the Twelve; 'as your leader, or president') that can only be President Brigham Young's, with no attribution line surviving the OCR between them -- still unresolved.
