Minutes of the June 1854 Special Conference

Conference record

1854 June General Conference·Whole Conference·1854-06-27

[This conference was conducted as an exception, held on the tenth anniversary of the martyrdom of the Prophet Joseph Smith. Hence the timing, the choice of speakers, and the choice of topics.] ¶

Minutes of a Special Conference of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints, held in the Tabernacle, Great Salt Lake City, June 27, 1854, at 10 a.m. ¶

Conference called to order by President Jedediah M. Grant. Singing. Prayer by Elder Ezra T. Benson. Singing. ¶

President Brigham Young preached upon being an Apostle of Joseph Smith. The choir sang, "Praise to the man who communed with Jehovah." ¶

The conference reconvened at 2 p.m., called to order by President Young. Singing. Prayer by Elder Orson Hyde. Singing. ¶

Elder John Taylor reviewed the scenes and transactions pertaining to the martyrdom of Joseph and Hyrum Smith in Carthage jail, when under the pledged faith of Governor Ford of Illinois that they should be protected. A hymn composed by Bernard Snow was sung. Benediction by Elder Ezra T. Benson. ¶

The conference reconvened June 28, at 10 a.m. Opened with singing. Prayer. Singing. ¶

President Heber C. Kimball bore testimony of Joseph Smith's being a Prophet of God. ¶

The following persons were appointed to go on missions: Elder John Taylor to the City of New York, and Nathaniel H. Felt, Alexander Robbins, Jeter Clinton, Martin H. Peck, and Abel Lamb, to accompany him. Elder John Young to the States. Lemon Brunson to Michigan. Preston Thomas and Enoch B. Tripp to Texas. Edwin Whiting to Ohio. James Craig to Ireland. ¶

John Smith, the oldest son of Hyrum Smith, was voted to be ordained Patriarch over the whole Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. ¶

President Jedediah M. Grant then delivered his "Apostasy, &c." discourse (filed separately as 1854-06-grant-apostasy.md). The Programme for the Fourth of July was read by Thomas Bullock. Chanting by the choir. Benediction by President Jedediah M. Grant. ¶

The conference reconvened at 2 p.m. Opened with singing. Prayer. Singing. ¶

President Jedediah M. Grant and Elder Lorenzo Snow bore testimony of Joseph Smith being a Prophet, and spoke on that subject. George Windows was voted to take a mission to England. ¶

President Brigham Young requested the Bishops to furnish lumber, etc., to build a bowery, so as to accommodate ten or twelve thousand persons comfortably. Chanting by the choir, "Judge me, O Lord, and I will give thanks unto thee." Benediction by President Young. ¶

Thomas Bullock, Clerk of Conference. George D. Watt, Reporter. ¶
