The Importance of the Mission to the Cotton Country

George A. Smith · 1861 October General Conference · 1861-10-08

Fidelity

◑Contemporaneous report

Published in Journal of Discourses, Volume 10, pp. 121-122. Verified directly against the Internet Archive scan/OCR of the printed JD volume (per_utah-and-the-mormons_journal-of-discourses-_brigham-young-_1863_10): the printed discourse heading itself reads 'made in the Tabernacle, Great Salt Lake City, October 8, 1862' — this is not a Weebly transcription error, it is the date as printed in JD. However, the surrounding conference minutes (both the Deseret News record and this discourse's own opening lines, which respond directly to remarks made 'during this Conference' about the cotton mission, immediately after Heber C. Kimball's Oct. 7, 1861 temple discourse and before George A. Smith's other Oct. 7, 1861 remarks recorded in the conference minutes) place its actual delivery within the October 1861 Semi-Annual Conference (Oct. 6-8, 1861), not October 1862 (there is no General Conference session dated October 8 specifically in either year's conference window per indexes/conference-calendar.md, but the narrative content is continuous with the 1861 conference business). The '1862' in the printed heading is treated as a JD printer/cataloging error and this file is dated 1861-10-08 accordingly. Reported by John V. Long; speaker did not review this text before publication. Full text reproduced; not abridged; page-by-page collated against IA OCR with no missing passages and a clean boundary (ends at 'Amen', followed by unrelated JD volume 10 material).
