Minutes of the April 1867 General Conference

Conference record · 1867 April General Conference · 1867-04-06

Fidelity

○Fragmentary

Deseret News minutes of the Thirty-Seventh Annual Conference, April 6-8, 1867. This is an unusually discourse-rich conference: all 13 discourses with surviving full text are filed separately as talks — Brigham Young's 'The Elders to Labor for the Unity of the Saints' and 'How the Sisters Can Help to Build Up the Kingdom' (Saturday AM, JD 11); John Taylor's 'Political and Social Economy' (Saturday PM, JD 11); George A. Smith's 'Raising Flax and Wool...' and Brigham Young's 'The Word of Wisdom' (Sunday AM; Smith in JD 11, Young in JD 12); Wilford Woodruff's 'Necessity of Unity in Faith and Practice,' Ezra T. Benson's 'Word of Wisdom—Happiness to Be Obtained...,' George Q. Cannon's 'Necessity of Union and Obedience to Counsel,' and Brigham Young's 'Every Saint on a Mission' (Sunday PM; Woodruff and Benson in JD 11, Cannon and Young in JD 12); Brigham Young's 'Building the Temple—Mormonism Embraces All Truth' (Monday AM, JD 11); and Charles C. Rich's 'Labor to Build Up the Kingdom,' Daniel H. Wells' 'Schools and School Teachers—Tithing, Etc.,' and Brigham Young's 'Education—Employment of Females' (Monday PM, JD 12). Two genuine gaps remain, confirmed against the Weebly page's own agenda and bibliography: President Heber C. Kimball spoke Saturday AM and Monday AM but both remarks survive only as third-person Deseret News summaries, never as a JD verbatim text, so no full-text talk file is possible; and President Daniel H. Wells delivered a separate discourse, 'The Kingdom of God Will Endure Forever,' Saturday PM (immediately after Taylor), which the Weebly bibliography attributes only to The Deseret News (May 1, 1867, pp. 138-139), not to JD — its full text was not located on the Weebly page consulted and it is not yet transcribed in this vault.
