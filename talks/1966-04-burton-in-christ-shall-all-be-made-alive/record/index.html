Heavyweight pass: verified source as the Church History Library-contributed Internet Archive scan conferencereport1966a (136th Annual Conference, held Apr 6, 9-10, 1966 — First Day on the actual Apr 6 founding anniversary, then Second/Third Day the following weekend; IA metadata confirms CHL call no. M204.1 A1 1880, 1897-). Rebuilt page reading order from the scan's DjVu XML word-coordinate data (scripts/ingest/cr-reading-order.py) and cross-checked every talk boundary against the report's own exhaustive back-of-book index (leaf n159-163), which lists every address with its own page range and sub-topic headings — an unusually detailed source for this conference that made it possible to identify and correctly merge extensive page-break fragmentation (this conference's original ingest had split most talks into two-to-four separate files). Confirmed session and exact date from the report's own dateline (Wednesday Apr 6 = First Day, Saturday Apr 9 = Second Day plus the Saturday-evening General Priesthood Meeting, Sunday Apr 10 = Third Day); corrected date_precision from month to exact and added the session field (both were previously unset). Added a page-anchored source_url (leaf n33 of conferencereport1966a) in place of the bare item link. This is Theodore M. Burton. The report's own back-of-book index (leaf n159) shows his address spanning leaf n32-35 with subject headings 'He redeemed all men,' 'The promise beyond the grave,' and 'Assembling Temple ordinance data,' but only the opening and closing portions were ever ingested; the middle portion (covering the first two of those headings) is missing from the source scan/OCR entirely and could not be recovered. The closing portion, previously misfiled as a separate talk under the nonexistent speaker 'Elray' (in a file whose real content has nothing to do with Elder ElRay L. Christiansen), was merged in as this talk's true ending; the gap between the two surviving fragments is marked explicitly rather than bridged with invented text. Editorial flag: Elder ElRay L. Christiansen's own address (index leaf n35-37, immediately following Burton's) was never ingested into the vault at all under any filename.
