This file preserves a complete transcription of Thomas Bullock's 17-leaf "General Conference minutes, Salt Lake City" (CR 100 318), Historian's Office general Church minutes, covering the October 1848 General Conference in the Bowery at Great Salt Lake City — the opening on Friday, October 6, and the adjourned Sunday sessions of October 8, 15, 22, and 29 — together with the revised fair copy of Brigham Young's October 8 address (leaves 14-17). Leaves are numbered 1-17 in catalog-viewer order (image files CR 100 318_2_11_1.jpg through _2_11_17.jpg). Two readings in this file's earlier partial transcription ("Special Conference," "10 1/2 A.M.") are corrected below against the scan. ¶

What else is known, per Jenson's Church Chronology

Source: Andrew Jenson, Church Chronology (1899). Jenson states that the Twelve Apostles' Dec. 5, 1847 council decision to reorganize the First Presidency was "ratified by the Church at a conference held in the Log Tabernacle, at Council Bluffs, Iowa, Dec. 27, 1847, and also at the general conference held in G. S. L. Valley, Oct. 8, 1848." The manuscript's own record of the Oct. 8 sustaining (leaf 1) corroborates this. ¶

Collation notes

  • Brigham Young's October 8 priesthood address survives in this folder in two witnesses: Bullock's minute-hand (leaves 1-4) and the revised fair copy (leaves 14-17, docketed "Epistle to Saints ... revised copied & sent" to O. Hyde and G. A. Smith at Kanesville). The fair copy resolves several tentative minute-hand readings ("hop over this stick"; "his Son George") and its revision layer — including a long cancelled passage on Oliver Cowdery — is preserved in the transcription.
  • The conference adjourned "to 6 April next," connecting this record to the April 1849 conference; the April 1848 Kanesville conference (see 1848-04-conference-record-log-tabernacle-conference.md) had likewise adjourned "until 6 Oct next," this conference.
