Session notes: President Smith's testimony, adjournment, and afternoon session opening

Conference record

1912 April General Conference·Second Day Afternoon Session·1912-04-06

President Joseph F. Smith bore a brief testimony: "I would just like to bear my testimony to you that the testimonies of my brethren, which have been so impressively spoken here today, and at other times, with reference to the divinity of the mission of the Prophet Joseph Smith, and of the gospel of Christ, and of the Son of God are true — for I know they are true." ¶

The choir sang the anthem "Daughter of Zion." ¶

President Smith read a telegram from John W. Young, expressing good-will to the Church and its leaders. ¶

Benediction was pronounced by Elder Stephen L. Chipman. ¶

Conference adjourned until 2 p.m. ¶

Conference was resumed at 2 p.m., President Joseph F. Smith presiding. ¶

The choir rendered a sacred chorus, "Adoration," by Beethoven. ¶

Prayer was offered by Elder Edward J. Wood. ¶

A bass solo, "Within the Holy Temple," was sung by Brother Alva Woodward. ¶
